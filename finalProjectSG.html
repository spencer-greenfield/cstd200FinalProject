In response, Yale chooses not just

to ignore the crisis in Gaza, not just

to ignore student protesters’ demands, but...

If I must die,

you must live

to tell my story

to sell my things

to buy a piece of cloth

This is a desperate, flawed attempt

by one Yale student

to tell other Yalies

the story of how and why we protest.

From Yale University to the ruins of Al Shifa,

I hope that whoever reads this

might be inspired to join us

in the fight for a free Palestine.

I have only seen peaceful protests, but…

I have been called antisemitic

as a Jewish person

participating in the protests, but…

We have been antagonized,

mischaracterized, and beaten,

but what matters most

is that our actions call attention

to the genocide in Gaza.

...to violently punish its students

when they speak up peacefully

to call for an end to war.

If you are reading this, Yalie,

and you disagree with the word

“genocide,”

 

consider that the

Al Shifa Hospital Complex

was smaller than the four square blocks

at the center of campus

that have contained Yale’s

main pro-Palestine protests since

the Occupy Beinecke movement

started on April 15, 2024.

 

See the image of Al Shifa after destruction.

...I see other students violently counterprotesting

around the country, blind to the reality of the situation

and receiving preferential treatment

from their administrations.

Dr. Alareer and his family

were murdered

in an airstrike

on his neighborhood in Gaza.

That is why we protest.

That is why we protest.

 

and some strings,

(make it white with a long tail)

I beg of you:

stand up,

and fly a kite with us.

45,000 sq. m

...I sing “Olam Chesed Yibaneh”

during a seder held in protest

against genocide.

 

It means “We will build this world

from love” in Hebrew.

The protesters are led by

Yale Jews for Ceasefire.

See it compared to how crowded

the 500-person protests

were in Beinecke Plaza.

 

so that a child, somewhere in Gaza

while looking heaven in the eye

Yale Police violently tackle a peaceful protester.

and bid no one farewell

not even to his flesh

not even to himself—
